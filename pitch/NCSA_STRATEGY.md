## 1. The new thesis (one sentence)

NCSA's funnel converts on emotion in a 30-minute sales call; a sport-specific
clutch/decision profile gives the rep a tangible, family-facing artifact that
lifts paid-tier conversion — and feeds richer per-sport data into Dr. Simpson's
Individual Development Plan (IDP) framework without displacing the MPA.

Two products living together:
- **MPA** — IMG's in-house, cross-sport, 5-factor psychometric, on-campus IDP input. Stays.
- **NTangible** — sport-specific, decision-making/clutch-specific, family-and-coach-facing artifact. New surface, new funnel.

The pitch is "we make your funnel convert better and your IDP richer," not
"we replace your psych product."

## 2. Why this lands with the post-EQT owners

EQT acquired IMG Academy in partnership with **Nord Anglia Education** (80+
premium international schools, 30 countries). EQT's stated growth thesis:

1. Apply the Nord Anglia playbook to IMG (scale globally, digitize).
2. **Asia expansion** — Singapore, India, Vietnam, Greater China.
3. Cross-pollinate IMG sport/wellbeing curriculum into Nord Anglia's school network.

IMG has already moved on the thesis: **Elevate by IMG Academy** launched
March 2026 as the B2B distribution vehicle for IMG's personal-development
curriculum into outside schools globally. SportsRecruits was acquired May
2025, consolidating recruiting under one P&L. Michael Branch was hired July
2025 expressly as VP Strategic Partnerships.

This is an owner group in *active growth-bet mode* with a freshly-staffed
partnerships function. The pitch should ride that wave, not introduce a new
strategic direction.

A sport-specific assessment that (a) plugs into NCSA's 4.5M-athlete funnel,
(b) drops cleanly into Elevate's school-licensing motion, and (c) is
internationally portable — sits directly on top of the EQT/Nord Anglia
thesis.

## 3. Where NTangible plugs into NCSA — three insertion points

NCSA's funnel:

1. Free profile created → 2. Automated email/phone outreach within hours →
3. "Free recruiting assessment" call → 4. Family conference call → 5. Sale
of a $1,300–$6,000 multi-year package → 6. Inside-coach concierge service →
7. Coach views on platform → 8. Commitment → 9. Testimonial / referral

We insert at:

- **Step 3 → 4 (conversion lift, primary value):** The rep sends a free
  sport-specific clutch profile as the artifact between the first call and
  the family conference call. Concrete, visual, "this is what your athlete
  *is*" — meaningfully different from a generic recruiting pitch. Even a
  1–2 point conversion lift on a $2,000 average package dwarfs the per-unit
  economics of the assessment itself. **This is the primary ROI story.**
- **Step 7 (coach value):** Mental profile rides alongside the athletic
  profile in the coach view. Differentiates NCSA from SportsRecruits (now
  also IMG-owned), FieldLevel, and the media plays (On3, Rivals, 247).
- **Step 5 (IDP feed):** Every NCSA paid family already gets IMG Academy+
  Essentials bundled in for year one ($120 retail). The NTangible profile
  becomes the per-sport input that makes Essentials *feel* personalized —
  and Simpson's team gets richer data flowing into the IDP without having
  to build sport-specific instruments themselves.

We do **not** pitch this as a new $10 SKU bolted onto checkout. We pitch it
as the **conversion-rate-optimization layer** for an existing $1.3K–$6K
funnel.

## 5. Commercial structure — rewritten

Drop the $10/$2/$1/$7 framing. It doesn't fit NCSA's package economics
and the "$1 to affiliate" line is moot — coaches are free users, not
revenue-share partners.

Three structures to put on the table, in order of preference:

### A. Conversion-share pilot (lead with this)
- NTangible delivered **free to the family** as part of the NCSA assessment-call workflow.
- IMG/NCSA pays a **per-completed-profile fee** (suggest $3–$5) for fulfillment.
- **Conversion-share kicker:** on top of the fulfillment fee, NTangible takes
  a share (suggest 3–5%) of incremental paid-package revenue from families
  who received a profile, measured against a holdout.
- This frames us as a CRO partner, not a vendor. EQT-owned PE shops respond
  to "measurable lift on existing funnel" language.

### B. Bundled-into-paid-tier
- Profile included in every paid NCSA tier (Champion through MVP+).
- Per-activation fee (~$7–$10) paid by NCSA.
- Lifts perceived value of paid tiers without raising sticker price.
- Lower upside than A; lower risk; easier procurement.

### C. Elevate co-distribution
- NTangible profile licensed into the **Elevate by IMG Academy** product
  for global school distribution alongside IMG's personal-development
  curriculum.
- Per-seat licensing or revenue share on Elevate contracts.
- This is the EQT/Nord Anglia international-thesis play. Park it for
  conversation 2 after the NCSA pilot is signed.

The $50K → $10K ad-reinvestment loop from v1 should stay on the menu but
move to a **secondary slide**. It's a fine flourish but it's not the
business case — the business case is funnel conversion.

## 6. The IMG Academy "pro bono" move — refined

Don't pitch "free academy access." Pitch:

> "The academy becomes the validation site. Every IMG athlete runs through
> the sport-specific profile alongside the MPA. Dr. Simpson's team owns
> the validation study. We publish a quarterly co-branded report on the
> incremental predictive value of sport-specific instruments on top of
> the MPA. NCSA gets to sell a product backed by ongoing peer-reviewed
> validation from its parent company's clinical staff — nobody else in
> recruiting has that."

This does three things:
1. Makes Duncan Simpson a **co-author**, not a defender of his own product.
2. Gives Lindsey Hamilton (Head of Mental Performance) a publishable
   research program.
3. Generates exactly the "valid, reliable" language that IMG already uses
   to describe the MPA — co-opted now for our profile.

Cost to us: ~zero. Marketing value: enormous.

## 10. Pitch language — mirror IMG's own voice

These phrases come straight out of IMG/NCSA marketing. Use them verbatim
in the deck so the pitch sounds native:

- "**Holistic, best-in-class**" — IMG's framing for the NCSA + SportsRecruits combo.
- "**Pivotal years of personal development**" — IMG's framing for the Academy+ bundle into NCSA. **Use this verbatim** when positioning the assessment.
- "**Valid, reliable snapshot**" — IMG's own MPA language. Mirror "valid" and "reliable" throughout — they care deeply about psychometric credibility.
- "**Individual Development Plan (IDP)**" — IMG's internal framework. Position the profile as a per-sport input *to* the IDP.
- "**Life-changing experiences, on campus and online**" — Brent Richard's tagline.
- "**Evidence-based**" / "**proven**" / "**trusted by top coaches and trainers**" — IMG Essentials language.
- "**Mental performance**" — never "sports psychology." IMG's consumer voice avoids the clinical term.
- "**Forging futures**" — recent campaign phrase.

Voice: confident, scale-bragging, evidence-citing, athlete-centric, future-
oriented. Lean toward "performance" and "development," away from clinical
or therapeutic language.

## 12. Success milestones

- **Pilot signed:** Option A, one sport, 90-day window, conversion-lift
  success metric agreed in writing, validation co-author letter signed
  by Simpson.
- **Pilot win condition:** statistically significant lift in free→paid
  conversion in pilot sport vs. holdout; positive qualitative feedback
  from ≥10 college coaches viewing profiles; first validation-study
  data published with IMG.
- **Expansion:** all 9 sports live in NCSA; move from pilot to standing
  conversion-share economics; formalize validation program as quarterly
  cadence.
- **Strategic:** NTangible profile becomes a standard field on every
  NCSA profile and a licensed module inside Elevate by IMG Academy.
  At that point we're infrastructure across both NCSA's 4.5M-athlete
  funnel and Nord Anglia's 80-school international network. That is
  the EQT-thesis outcome and the right framing for any future
  acquisition conversation.
